## Where Topview AI Is Less Ideal

The trade-offs are straightforward.

Topview AI is less ideal if:

- your real bottleneck is offer strategy, not creative production
- your team does not yet know what kind of ad narrative works
- you mainly need writing, research, or automation instead of video
- you expect the tool to replace human creative direction entirely

This is still a tool, not a business strategy.

If the message, offer, and channel are weak, more AI-generated ad variations will not fix the core problem.

## What To Check Before You Commit

Before you adopt Topview AI, ask a few practical questions.

### 1. Is your bottleneck really video production?

If the main problem is targeting or conversion, more video capacity may not move the result much.

### 2. Do you already know your winning ad structure?

Reference-based workflows are most useful when you already understand what kind of creative logic you want to reproduce.

### 3. Do you need one-off assets or repeatable creative throughput?

Topview AI looks easier to justify when the need is ongoing testing, not one isolated campaign.

### 4. Will it replace friction or add another tool?

The best-case outcome is less production friction. The worst case is another dashboard where drafts pile up without improving execution.

### 5. Is your team ready to review and refine output?

The tool may accelerate drafts, but teams still need judgment around script quality, brand fit, claims, and channel context.
